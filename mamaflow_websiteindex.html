The Science

Built on clinical evidence.
Not assumptions.

MamaFlow's framework is grounded in published postpartum guidelines — and designed to address the gaps mainstream apps ignore.

🏥
RCOG & ACOG Guidelines

Exercise phases built on Royal College of Obstetricians and ACOG evidence. Vaginal and C-section pathways are completely separate — because they are medically different recoveries.

🧮
Mifflin-St Jeor Personalisation

Calorie targets calculated from your actual height, weight, and age using the most validated formula for postpartum women — adjusted daily for sleep deprivation, breastfeeding, and activity.

🤱
Breastfeeding Safety Built In

Breastfeeding users never see a calorie deficit recommendation. An additional 400–500 kcal is applied automatically. Calorie restriction while breastfeeding is never an option in this app.

🌍
Ethnicity-Aware Modelling

Standard equations were built from studies of White European populations. MamaFlow applies evidence-based BMR adjustments for Black and South Asian women — the first postpartum app to do this.

🔬
Under Clinical Review

The MamaFlow clinical framework is being reviewed by a registered women's health physiotherapist before public launch. We will not launch until it is signed off.

🔒
GDPR Compliant by Design

Health data is the most sensitive data category under UK GDPR. MamaFlow is ICO-registered. All data is stored on EU servers. You own your data and can delete it at any time.

Questions

Everything you want to know.

Honest answers — no marketing spin. If your question isn't here, email us.

What exactly is the daily check-in and how long does it take?
+
The morning check-in is five questions: how you slept, your energy level, how your body feels, your emotional state, and whether you're breastfeeding today. It takes under 90 seconds. From those five answers, the AI pivot engine calculates your state for the day and builds a personalised workout recommendation and calorie target. That's it. No lengthy forms, no meal logging, no tracking every rep.
I've never exercised before. Is this app really for me?
+
Yes — and we mean that. MamaFlow was specifically designed to be accessible to women with no fitness background. During onboarding you tell us your experience level: New to fitness, Some experience, or Active before pregnancy. That choice changes everything downstream — including how workouts are explained to you. Beginners get full step-by-step descriptions of every movement, written in plain English with no assumed knowledge. "Gentle mobility flow" becomes "five simple movements, mostly on the floor, here's exactly what to do." Nobody gets left behind.
What is a "pivot state" and how does it change my plan?
+
Your daily check-in maps to one of nine pivot states — a combination of your sleep score and energy level. Each state has a different workout type (from breathwork and rest on State 1, through to full strength sessions on States 7–9), a different calorie target, and a different message tone. State 1 means your body is asking for stillness and we honour that without judgment. State 9 means you slept well and your energy is high — let's use it. The same woman can be in State 1 on Tuesday and State 7 on Thursday. The app moves with her.
What's the difference between the app and just using ChatGPT?
+
Five things ChatGPT cannot do that MamaFlow can. Memory: MamaFlow knows your delivery type, your recovery phase, your fitness level, your history — and uses all of it every day. ChatGPT forgets you the moment you close the chat. Clinical safety: The postpartum exercise framework is built on RCOG and ACOG evidence. ChatGPT has no clinical guardrails. Habit system: The check-in streak, the daily plan, the push notifications — MamaFlow is designed to be a daily habit, not a one-off query. No setup tax: ChatGPT requires you to re-enter all your context every single morning. MamaFlow gives you your plan in 90 seconds. Your specific data: MamaFlow calculates from your real BMR. ChatGPT guesses.

Does MamaFlow work for all fitness levels and backgrounds?
+
Yes. The three fitness levels — new to fitness, some experience, and active before pregnancy — change the language, depth, and format of everything the app shows you. A former marathon runner and a woman who has never worked out in her life both get plans that are clinically appropriate for their postpartum recovery stage. The difference is how the plan is communicated and the starting intensity. MamaFlow is not a performance app for athletes. It is a recovery and wellness app that grows with you at whatever pace your body allows.
Is MamaFlow safe to use if I had a caesarean section?
+
Yes — and C-section recovery has a completely separate pathway in MamaFlow, not a modified version of the same thing. A caesarean is major abdominal surgery through seven layers of tissue. The recovery is fundamentally different from a vaginal birth. In the first six weeks, the C-section pathway restricts any trunk rotation, lifting beyond infant weight, or sustained standing exercise. From six weeks it moves through a carefully staged programme that includes scar tissue awareness and specific exercises that support the healing of the lower abdominal wall. You tell us your delivery type during onboarding and the app handles the rest.
My doctor cleared me at 6 weeks. Can I jump straight into intense exercise?
+
No — and this is one of the most important things MamaFlow communicates. Medical clearance at 6 weeks confirms sufficient wound healing. It does not confirm readiness for high-intensity exercise. The pelvic floor, the deep core, the hormonal environment — none of these have fully recovered at 6 weeks for the vast majority of women. MamaFlow applies a structured return-to-movement protocol starting from medical clearance, beginning with breathwork and pelvic floor activation before progressing to loaded movement. "Cleared" means you can start the process. It does not mean the process is finished.
I'm still postpartum but my baby is over 12 months. Is MamaFlow for me?
+
Absolutely. "Postpartum" is not a 12-week window — it is a phase that can extend for years, particularly for women who experienced significant birth injuries, multiple pregnancies close together, or prolonged breastfeeding. MamaFlow serves mums of children 0–5 years old. If your body is still in recovery, you are still in the right place. The app will ask for your baby's age and adjust the phase accordingly — but it never tells you that your timeline is wrong.
What guidelines is the exercise framework based on?
+
The MamaFlow framework is grounded in the RCOG (Royal College of Obstetricians and Gynaecologists) postpartum guidelines, the ACOG (American College of Obstetricians and Gynecologists) 2020 Committee Opinion on physical activity postpartum, the POGP (Pelvic, Obstetric and Gynaecological Physiotherapy) guidance on returning to running, and published evidence on diastasis recti rehabilitation from Mota et al. and Lee & Hodges. The full clinical framework is currently under review by a registered women's health physiotherapist. A clinical briefing document has been prepared for endorsement before launch.
I have diastasis recti. Can I use MamaFlow?
+
Yes, with care. MamaFlow asks about symptoms during onboarding and the programme avoids exercises that are contraindicated for diastasis recti (crunches, sit-ups, double-leg raises, and loaded trunk flexion). The core reconnection pathway focuses on transverse abdominis activation, pelvic floor coordination, and breathing mechanics — all evidence-based approaches for DR rehabilitation. However, MamaFlow is not a substitute for one-to-one physiotherapy if your diastasis is significant. The app will always recommend consulting a women's health physio if you flag significant symptoms.
How is my calorie target calculated? Is it just an estimate?
+
It is calculated from your specific data using the Mifflin-St Jeor equation — the most validated BMR formula for healthy adults including postpartum women, selected over the Harris-Benedict equation based on published accuracy studies (Frankenfield et al., 2005). The equation uses your height, weight, and age. This gives a Basal Metabolic Rate, which is then multiplied by your activity level to give TDEE. On top of that, daily adjustments are applied for sleep deprivation (up to +220 kcal for under 4 hours sleep), breastfeeding (+400–500 kcal for exclusive feeding), and training intensity. It is the most personalised calorie calculation available in a consumer postpartum app.
The calorie number looks too high. Should I be worried?
+
This is one of the most common reactions — and it is almost always diet culture talking, not your body. The postpartum period significantly increases your calorie needs compared to what most women have been told to eat. You are recovering from major physical trauma, your hormones are shifting, and if you're breastfeeding you are producing an entirely separate food supply for another human being. MamaFlow will never set a calorie target that is medically inappropriate. If it feels high, that is worth sitting with. The food translation feature shows you what the number looks like in real meals — which often makes it feel more accessible and less clinical.
I'm breastfeeding. Will MamaFlow push me to lose weight?
+
No — this is a hard rule in the app, not a setting. Calorie deficit states are disabled for all actively breastfeeding users. The evidence (Lovelady et al., 2000; McCrory et al., 1999) shows that significant calorie restriction during active breastfeeding risks reducing milk supply and impairs maternal recovery. MamaFlow adds 400–500 kcal to your daily target for exclusive breastfeeding and 200–250 kcal for combination feeding. Weight management goals are available as a focus only once you have fully weaned. Until then, the app focuses on fuelling recovery and sustaining your feeding relationship.
Do I have to count calories? I find that stressful.
+
No. During onboarding, MamaFlow asks how you'd like to see your nutrition. You can choose: show me what it looks like in food (a sample day of real meals, no numbers shown), show me the number with food context (both the calorie target and the food translation), or just give me the numbers (for those comfortable with tracking). The calorie calculation still happens in the background in all modes — it drives the meal suggestions and the daily adjustments — but you never have to see a number if that doesn't work for you. You can also dismiss the food translation at any time and restore it later in settings.
Does MamaFlow provide meal plans or recipes?
+
In Stage 0 and Stage 1, MamaFlow provides a sample day of eating — a visual guide to what your calorie and macro targets look like in real food across four meals. It is illustrative, not prescriptive. You are not expected to eat exactly this. The goal is to make the numbers feel tangible. Full recipe guidance and a more detailed meal planning feature are on the roadmap for Stage 4, informed by what beta users tell us they want. If meal planning is important to you, let us know in your feedback — it directly influences what we build next.

My health data is sensitive. How is it protected?
+
Health data is the most sensitive personal data category under UK GDPR (Article 9 special category data). MamaFlow treats it accordingly. All data is stored in encrypted EU-region Supabase infrastructure with Row Level Security — meaning only you can access your own data. MamaFlow is registered with the Information Commissioner's Office (ICO) as a data controller. We will never sell your data, share it with advertisers, or use it to train general AI models without your explicit consent. A full Privacy Policy is available at the bottom of this page.
Can I delete my account and all my data?
+
Yes — always. Account deletion is available from within the app's settings with a single button. Deleting your account permanently removes all personal data, health data, and check-in history from our servers within 30 days. This is your right under the UK GDPR right to erasure (Article 17). If you have any difficulty deleting your account, you can also request deletion by emailing us directly and we will process it within the statutory timeframe.
Does MamaFlow share data with third parties?
+
MamaFlow uses a small number of trusted third-party services to operate: Supabase (database and authentication, EU servers), Anthropic Claude API (AI pivot engine — inputs are processed but not stored by Anthropic), Mux (video delivery), RevenueCat/Stripe (payment processing), and PostHog (anonymised usage analytics). None of these services receive personally identifiable health data. No data is ever shared with advertising networks, data brokers, or third parties for commercial purposes. The full list of data processors is disclosed in our Privacy Policy.
Will my data be used to train the AI?
+
Your individual personal data is never used to train the underlying Claude AI model — that is Anthropic's own model and MamaFlow has no control over or access to its training data. MamaFlow may use aggregated, anonymised patterns from user behaviour (e.g. which pivot states correlate with highest session completion rates) to improve the recommendations the app makes. This analysis never involves personally identifiable data and will only happen with proper anonymisation practices in place. Any use of data beyond app operation will be clearly disclosed and subject to your rights under UK GDPR.
